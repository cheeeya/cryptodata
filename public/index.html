What is Cryptocurrency?

A cryptocurrency is a form of digital currency built on a decentralized network. Because there is no central server, all transactions must be broadcast on the network and validated by peers.

The term cryptocurrency comes from the validation process, which requires the computing of cryptographic hash functions. "Miners" provide the heavy processing power required to produce the hashes and are thus rewarded with cryptocoins.

Once validated and secured, transactions are permanently added to the blockchain where they are readily available to the public.
